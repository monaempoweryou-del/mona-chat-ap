# MONA Business Intelligence Engine — Canonical Framework
## Version 1.0 | June 5, 2026

> **Status:** Active framework. Built through simultaneous build + live test (VALIDATE-001 principle).
> **First live test:** Goldsmith Financial — June 5, 2026
> **Supersedes:** MONA_Lead_Onboarding_Workflow_June2026.md (conceptual predecessor)
>
> **Core principle:** The goal is not to collect information. The goal is to understand the business, understand the owner, understand the market — deeply enough to produce intelligence that is impossible to ignore.

---

## Philosophy

A prospect who receives a generic marketing report becomes a prospect.
A prospect who receives a report that makes them feel truly understood becomes a client.

The difference is not the quality of the writing. The difference is whether the document reveals something the owner already knows but has never seen articulated — and whether it reveals something they didn't know but immediately recognizes as true.

**Research first. Ask second. Understand first. Recommend second.**

---

## The 9-Step Process

| Step | Name | Input | Output |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Lead Arrives | Incoming email | Extracted intelligence profile |
| 2 | Understand The Human | Gap questions only | Owner psychology + goals confirmed |
| 3 | Research The Business | All available public sources | Digital footprint audit |
| 4 | Identify Bottlenecks | Research findings + owner input | Gap and risk analysis |
| 5 | Generate Business Intelligence | All data synthesized | Intelligence brief |
| 6 | Create Strategic Recommendations | Intelligence brief | Prioritized recommendation set |
| 7 | Build Execution Plan | Recommendations | 90-day roadmap |
| 8 | Generate Proposal | Roadmap + pricing | Branded HTML proposal |
| 9 | Follow-Up System | Delivery confirmation | 3-touch follow-up sequence |

## Step 1 — Lead Arrives

### Purpose
Extract maximum intelligence from the initial email before asking the prospect for anything.

### Extract From Every Email

| Category | Fields |
|----------|--------|
| Identity | Business name, owner name, title |
| Industry | Vertical, sub-vertical, regulatory environment |
| Location | City, state, geography served |
| Digital | Website URL, social URLs, any platforms mentioned |
| Goals | Stated objectives, time horizons, success metrics |
| Problems | Stated frustrations, current challenges |
| Context | History with marketing, competitor awareness |
| Tone | How they write (formal/casual, confident/uncertain, detailed/vague) |

### Decision: Understanding Phase Depth

**The Understanding phase is adaptive.** The richness of the initial email determines how many questions Step 2 requires.

| Initial Email Quality | Step 2 Approach |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| Minimal (name + website only) | Full 7-question understanding interview |
| Moderate (some goals, some context) | 4-5 targeted gap questions |
| Detailed (goals, target, frustrations, URLs) | 2-4 specific gap questions only |
| Exceptional (complete strategic brief) | 1-2 highly specific questions about missing intelligence |

**Framework Discovery (Session 001 — Goldsmith Financial):** Some leads arrive with exceptional briefs that contain more strategic clarity than most agencies ask for. Do not run a generic 5-question template on a lead who already answered all 5. Instead, identify the specific intelligence gaps that remain and ask only those.

### Industry Flag: Financial Services
Financial advisors are regulated by FINRA and the SEC. This triggers a mandatory compliance check before any marketing recommendations:
- Testimonials/endorsements require specific disclosures (Reg BI, FINRA 2210)
- Performance claims are restricted
- "Best," "top," and superlative language requires substantiation
- Client lists and AUM figures have disclosure requirements

Add a compliance flag to the Step 1 profile for: financial advisors, attorneys, healthcare providers, real estate brokers, insurance agents, and any other licensed professional.

## Step 2 — Understand The Human

### Purpose
Before researching the business, understand the owner's psychology, prior failures, and the gap between where they believe they are and where they want to be. This shapes the entire tone and framing of the intelligence report.

### Standard Question Bank (select only what's missing from Step 1)

**About the owner's goals:**
- When you imagine this is working — not in 12 months, but in 90 days — what specifically would you see that tells you things are moving?
- What does success look like to you personally, beyond the business metrics?

**About current reality:**
- How do most new clients find you today? (referrals, online search, networking, other)
- What's working well enough that you wouldn't want us to touch it?
- What has frustrated you most about marketing or your online presence?

**About prior attempts:**
- Have you worked with a marketing agency or consultant before? What happened?
- What have you already tried that didn't work? (saves us recommending what they've rejected)

**About constraints:**
- Are there professional or regulatory guidelines that affect what you can say publicly in your marketing?
- Is there anything you absolutely do not want to do? (video, social media, certain platforms)

**About the engagement:**
- What's your budget range for marketing over the next 12 months?
- Do you want to be involved in content creation or would you prefer a fully managed approach?

### Response Email Template

```
Subject: Re: [Business Name] — [Confirming Assessment Underway / Follow-Up]

[First name],

Thank you for [the detailed overview / reaching out / taking the time] — [one genuine specific 
observation about something they wrote that demonstrates you actually read it].

We've [started the research / reviewed everything carefully] and [one concrete thing you're 
already doing — makes them feel you're working, not waiting].

Before we finalize the assessment, [N] specific questions:

[Only the gap questions — no more than 4. Numbered. Short. Purposeful.]

[These answers will help us / This will allow us to] [specific benefit tied to their stated goal].

[Warm close — professional but human. No "Best regards." No agency jargon.]

[Signature]
```

### What to Capture From Step 2 Response

| Field | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|
| Current client acquisition source | Tells you what's actually working — don't fix what isn't broken |
| Prior marketing failures | Tells you what to avoid recommending |
| Regulatory constraints | Prevents compliance violations in recommendations |
| 90-day success definition | Gives you the right short-term KPIs for the execution plan |
| Budget range | Allows the proposal to be realistic, not aspirational |
| Content involvement preference | Determines whether strategy is fully managed or collaborative |

## Step 3 — Research The Business

### Research Protocol

Execute in this sequence. Document every finding — both what exists and what is absent.

**Tier 1 — Direct Sources (always check)**

| Source | What to Find |
|--------|-------------|
| Website (WebFetch or WebSearch) | All pages indexed, headline/messaging, CTAs, contact info, mobile-friendliness signals, copyright date |
| Google Business Profile | Rating, review count, review recency, completeness score, posts, photos, Q&A |
| FINRA BrokerCheck / SEC IAPD | (financial advisors only) CRD number, clean/disclosed record, registration history |
| CFP Board / professional credentials | Certification status, active vs. lapsed |

**Tier 2 — Visibility & Directory Presence**

| Source | What to Find |
|--------|-------------|
| Yelp | Rating, review count, address accuracy (NAP consistency) |
| Facebook | Page likes, post frequency, review count, profile completeness |
| LinkedIn Company | Followers, post frequency, employee count shown |
| LinkedIn Personal (owner) | Connections, posts, recommendations, engagement |
| Instagram | Follower count, post count, last post date, content quality |
| Apple Maps | Presence, address accuracy |
| Bing Places | Presence, accuracy |
| Nextdoor | Listing presence, address shown |

**Tier 3 — Authority & Reputation**

| Source | What to Find |
|--------|-------------|
| Industry review platforms | Indyfin, WiserAdvisor, SmartAsset (advisors); Houzz (contractors); etc. |
| Podcast/media mentions | Any appearances, interviews, guest articles |
| Referral partner pages | Any partner websites that list/link to them |
| Local business directories | ChamberofCommerce, ShopIn[City], local listings |
| Crunchbase / ZoomInfo | Company profile presence and data accuracy |

**Tier 4 — Competitive Landscape**

| Source | What to Find |
|--------|-------------|
| Google SERP (their primary keyword) | Who ranks above them? Who appears in the map pack? |
| Competitor websites | Messaging, positioning, services, perceived professionalism vs. the client |
| Review comparison | Their review count vs. top competitors |

### Research Blockers

| Blocker | Alternative Path |
|---------|-----------------|
| WebFetch 403 (bot protection) | WebSearch site: query to get indexed pages and snippet content |
| No GBP access (no API) | Note from Maps URL + visual review in Step 5 |
| Private social profiles | Note as gap — inability to verify is a finding |
| Regulatory databases blocked | WebSearch for CRD number + "BrokerCheck" to surface public record summaries |

**Framework Discovery (Session 001):** WebFetch will fail on many professional services websites (financial advisors, attorneys, medical practices) — they use enterprise hosting/WAF that blocks bots. WebSearch + site: queries are the primary fallback. Google's indexed content is often sufficient to assess messaging quality.

**Framework Discovery (Session 001):** If only 1 page of a website appears in `site:domain.com` search results, the website has critical SEO indexing issues — Google cannot crawl it. This is immediately a top-3 finding regardless of the business type.

### NAP Consistency Check

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Inconsistency across directories is a local SEO penalty.

For every business, compare these four fields across every directory found:
- Business name (exact spelling and legal name)
- Address (including suite number, city, state, zip)
- Phone number (area code)
- Website URL

Flag every discrepancy as a finding.

## Step 4 — Identify Bottlenecks

### Framework

For each finding from Step 3, classify:

| Classification | Definition | Priority |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| Critical Blocker | Actively suppressing visibility, trust, or conversions | Fix first |
| High Impact Gap | Significant opportunity being missed | Fix second |
| Quick Win | Small effort, meaningful improvement | Fix third |
| Strategic Opportunity | Requires planning but high long-term value | Roadmap item |
| Non-Issue | Present but not a meaningful problem | Note only |

### The Gap Between Belief and Reality

Always document:
- What the owner believes is the problem
- What the research reveals is the actual problem

These are often different. The report's credibility depends on surfacing this distinction respectfully and with evidence.

## Step 5 — Generate Business Intelligence

### Report Structure (HTML)

```
[Header — Business name, owner name, MONA branding]

EXECUTIVE INTELLIGENCE BRIEF
One paragraph. What the research found. What it means for growth.

SECTION 1 — OWNER PROFILE
What Joshua told us. His goals. His target market. His positioning intent.
[Written back to them in MONA's voice — shows we listened]

SECTION 2 — CURRENT DIGITAL REALITY
What the research found. Each platform. Each finding.
[Evidence-based, not opinion]

SECTION 3 — GAP ANALYSIS
Belief vs. Reality table.
What they think is working vs. what the research confirms.
What they may not know is missing.

SECTION 4 — BOTTLENECK MAP
Critical blockers → Quick wins → Strategic opportunities
[Ranked by impact]

SECTION 5 — COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Who they're competing against. What competitors are doing.
Where there is a clear opening.

SECTION 6 — KEY OPPORTUNITIES
Top 3-5 specific, actionable opportunities.
Each with: what it is, why it matters, expected impact.
[Never generic. Always specific to this business.]
```

### Tone Standard

The report should make the owner think:
*"They understand my business better than most people who have worked in it."*

Every finding must be specific. Every recommendation must be tied to their stated goal. Never use language that could apply to any business.

## Step 6 — Create Strategic Recommendations

Each recommendation must include:
- **What:** The specific action
- **Why:** Connected explicitly to their stated goal
- **Expected Impact:** What changes when this is done
- **Time to Impact:** When they should expect to see results
- **Complexity:** Simple / Moderate / Complex

Never recommend more than 7 items. Prioritized lists that exceed 7 become overwhelm, not strategy.

---

## Step 7 — Build Execution Plan

### 90-Day Roadmap Structure

| Phase | Timing | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Quick Wins | Days 1–30 | Highest impact, lowest effort. Things that show immediate momentum. |
| Foundation | Days 31–60 | Core infrastructure — things that make everything else work better. |
| Growth Engine | Days 61–90 | Activations that compound over time. |

For each item in each phase:
- Owner action required (if any)
- MONA action (what we handle)
- Expected result
- How to measure it

---

## Step 8 — Generate Proposal

### Proposal Principles
- Only recommend services that are justified by the research findings
- Do not include services not referenced in the recommendations
- Price anchored to the client's stated outcome goal, not to MONA's service menu
- Scope is explicit — both what is included and what is not

### Proposal Structure (HTML)
1. Executive summary (2-3 sentences)
2. What we found (brief research highlights)
3. What we recommend (tied to findings)
4. How we'll execute (simplified 90-day milestones)
5. Investment (tiered if appropriate)
6. What's next (clear single CTA)

---

## Step 9 — Follow-Up System

Three-touch post-delivery sequence. All drafted as Gmail drafts at time of report delivery.

| Touch | Timing | Purpose | Tone |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| Touch 1 | Day 2 | Check-in: Did they receive it? Any questions? | Warm, brief |
| Touch 2 | Day 7 | One specific question about their reaction to a finding | Curious, engaged |
| Touch 3 | Day 14 | Final outreach — offer a call to walk through the proposal | Direct, respectful |

After Touch 3: if no response, move to archive. Do not follow up beyond Day 14 without a specific signal of interest.

## Compliance Flags by Industry

| Industry | Compliance Consideration |
|---------|------------------------|
| Financial Advisors (FINRA/SEC) | Testimonials require disclosures. No performance guarantees. "CFP®" is a registered mark with usage rules. Reg BI applies. |
| Attorneys | State bar advertising rules. No "best" or superlatives without substantiation. |
| Healthcare / Doctors | HIPAA. No patient testimonials without consent. |
| Real Estate | State DRE rules. Disclosure requirements on marketing materials. |
| Contractors | License number required in advertising in most states. |
| Insurance Agents | State DOI rules. Product-specific marketing restrictions. |

## Compliance Flags — Financial Advisors (Updated Session 004)

> The 2022 SEC Marketing Rule changed the compliance landscape significantly. Update all financial advisor BIE engagements to reflect the following:

| Compliance Area | Old Rule | Current Rule (2022+) |
|----------------|---------|---------------------|
| Client testimonials | Prohibited | Now permitted with required disclosures |
| Testimonial disclosures | N/A | Must state: (1) whether person is a current client, (2) whether compensated, (3) any material conflicts |
| Testimonial performance claims | Prohibited | Still prohibited — testimonials must reflect experience, not returns |
| LPL-specific process | N/A | Submit to LPL ClientWorks MRR for advertising use. Organic Google reviews do not require pre-approval. |
| 2026 SEC enforcement focus | N/A | Scrutinizing testimonial disclosure completeness — ensure disclosures are "clear and prominent" |

**Recommendation for all financial advisor BIE reports:** Include a section on the testimonial opportunity. Most advisors are operating under the old (prohibited) assumption. The SEC Marketing Rule changed this in 2022. Presenting this as an untapped opportunity is high-value positioning for MONA.

## Framework Discoveries — Session 001 (Goldsmith Financial — Initial Build)

| Discovery | Implication for Framework |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Lead sent an exceptional brief — detailed goals, target market, complete digital footprint | Understanding phase is adaptive. Match question depth to information quality. Never run a generic questionnaire on a detailed lead. |
| Financial services websites block WebFetch (403) | WebSearch + site: query is primary research path for regulated industries. WebFetch is a nice-to-have, not a dependency. |
| Only 1 page indexed by Google (goldsmithfinancial.net) | Single-page indexing is a top-3 finding for any business. Always check with site: query first. |
| Two "Joshua Goldsmith CFP" profiles exist on LinkedIn | Brand fragmentation across duplicate profiles is a common finding. Always check for name collision — especially for personal brand businesses. |
| Old San Diego address appears on Yelp and Nextdoor despite current Temecula location | NAP inconsistency after a location move is extremely common. Stale directory listings are always an early audit finding. |
| No Google reviews visible for a 20-year veteran advisor | Absence of reviews is a systemic problem for service professionals who rely on relationship-based business. Review generation strategy is almost always a recommendation. |
| Clean FINRA record = under-utilized marketing asset | For regulated professionals, a clean compliance record is a trust signal that almost none of them actively market. Surface this as an opportunity. |
| Home address (30463 Danube Ct) appears to be residential | Address matters for professional perception in high-trust services. Flag for discussion — virtual office or executive suite may be appropriate. |

## Framework Discoveries — Session 004 (Goldsmith Financial — Autonomous Execution)

| Discovery | Implication for Framework |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| BIE Steps 1–9 run almost entirely autonomously | Full workflow — email reading → research → report → follow-up drafts — completes without Maor input. One genuine blocker: Gmail MCP cannot send (draft only). Minimum human touchpoint = 1 send click. |
| Full BIE report can be completed without Step 2 responses | Initial email intelligence is sufficient for a complete, deliverable report. Step 2 answers refine the strategic layer but don't block the core deliverable. Do not wait — deliver the report, note the 4 gaps, refine after responses arrive. |
| Gmail MCP limitation: read and draft only, no send capability | Permanent system constraint until a send tool is added. Workflow design must account for this: create draft → Maor clicks send. This is the single remaining human touchpoint in an otherwise autonomous workflow. |
| Competitive landscape requires 3+ search rounds to surface completely | First search finds 4–5 competitors. Additional keyword combinations reveal 3–4 more. Always run at least 3 searches with different query structures before declaring the competitive set complete. |
| NAP audit: explicitly search "[Business Name] + [all known previous cities]" | Stale listings in old cities don't show up in current-city searches. For Goldsmith, searching "Goldsmith Financial San Diego" surfaced the Yelp listing with outdated address — invisible in Temecula-based searches. Add to standard NAP audit protocol. |
| LPL financial advisor testimonials: NOW AVAILABLE under 2022 SEC Marketing Rule | Most LPL advisors are still operating under the old prohibited assumption. The 2022 SEC Marketing Rule changed this. Testimonials allowed with disclosures via LPL ClientWorks MRR process. Present this as an untapped opportunity in all financial advisor BIE reports. |
| Follow-up drafts: create all 3 at time of report delivery, not as a separate step | No dependency on Step 2 or any other input. Sequence: Day 2 (step 2 nudge), Day 7 (compelling preview finding), Day 14 (report complete, offer delivery). Create immediately after BIE report. |
| Include "Open Intelligence Gaps" section in every BIE report | Instead of withholding report until all questions answered, deliver with a gaps section. Makes Step 2 valuable (refines report) rather than required (blocks report). Reduces time-to-value. |
