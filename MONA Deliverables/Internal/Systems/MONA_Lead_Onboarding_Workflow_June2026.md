# MONA Digital Marketing — Master Lead Onboarding Workflow
## Effective: June 5, 2026

> **Status:** Approved workflow definition. Implementation pending gap analysis review.
> **Governing principle:** Research first. Ask second. Understand first. Recommend second.

---

## Trigger

A new lead email arrives in the inbox.

---

## Primary Objective

Collect the minimum information necessary from the lead while performing the maximum amount of research ourselves.

The customer should feel like they are dealing with a highly capable team that understands their business, respects their time, and does the heavy lifting for them.

The goal is not to gather information. The goal is to understand the business deeply enough to produce a strategic report that demonstrates expertise, builds trust, and naturally leads toward becoming a client.

---

## Step 1 — Identify the Lead

Analyze the incoming email.

Determine:
- Business name
- Industry
- Services
- Location
- Website (if provided)
- Social profiles (if provided)
- Any goals, frustrations, or opportunities mentioned

Extract every possible piece of information before asking the lead for anything.

---

## Step 2 — Initial Response

Respond professionally and warmly.

Thank the lead for reaching out.

Explain that we would like to provide a complimentary business visibility and marketing assessment.

The purpose of the assessment is to help them understand:
- What is working
- What is not working
- Missed opportunities
- Areas for improvement
- Potential growth opportunities

Request only the minimum information required:
1. Website URL (if applicable)
2. Social media links
3. What is currently working well in your marketing?
4. What is currently not working well?
5. What is your biggest business goal over the next 12 months?

Do not overwhelm the lead with questions. Keep the process simple and easy.

---

## Step 3 — Research Phase

After receiving the information, perform maximum research using every available source.

Research areas:
- Website (structure, UX, content quality, load speed, mobile experience)
- SEO (on-page, technical, keyword positioning, local signals)
- Google Business Profile (completeness, reviews, posts, rankings)
- Social Media (presence, engagement, content quality, consistency)
- Branding (visual identity, messaging consistency, value proposition clarity)
- Online reputation (reviews across platforms, response patterns)
- Competitors (who ranks above them, what they're doing differently)
- Market positioning (how the business is perceived vs. how it wants to be perceived)
- Customer journey (from discovery → contact → conversion)
- Visibility (where the business appears and where it doesn't)
- Conversion opportunities (friction points in the current funnel)

Do not ask the customer for information that can be obtained through research.

---

## Step 4 — Analysis Phase

Identify:
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Opportunities
- Risks
- Bottlenecks
- Missed opportunities
- Quick wins (results achievable within 30 days)
- Long-term opportunities (6–12 month horizon)

Determine where the business is today versus where the owner wants it to be.

---

## Step 5 — Strategic Recommendation

Create a customized report.

The report must demonstrate:
- Deep understanding of the business
- Deep understanding of the owner's goals
- Deep understanding of the marketplace
- Deep understanding of the marketing opportunities

Every recommendation must be specific to this business. Avoid generic recommendations.

---

## Step 6 — Execution Plan

Create a realistic implementation roadmap.

Prioritize:
- Highest ROI opportunities
- Fastest wins
- Most impactful improvements

For each item explain:
- What should be done
- Why it should be done
- Expected impact
- Recommended order of execution

---

## Step 7 — Proposal Development

Create a proposal that is:
- Fair to the client
- Profitable for MONA
- Aligned with the business goals
- Focused on realistic outcomes

Never overpromise. Only recommend services that genuinely improve the client's position.

---

## Communication Standards

Every response must make the customer feel: understood, valued, respected, and supported.

The customer should leave every interaction thinking:
- "These people understand my business."
- "They know what they're talking about."
- "They've done their homework."
- "They're making this easy for me."

---

## Guiding Principle

The customer should do as little work as possible. MONA should do as much work as possible.

**Research first. Ask second. Understand first. Recommend second.**

The onboarding process should feel effortless for the customer while producing maximum business intelligence for MONA.
